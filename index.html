Abstract

Video foundation models have significantly advanced embodied intelligence, unlocking new possibilities for generating diverse robot data that capture perception, reasoning, and action in the physical world. However, synthesizing high-quality videos that accurately reflect real-world robotic interactions remains challenging, and the lack of a standardized benchmark limits fair comparisons and progress. To address this gap, we introduce a comprehensive robotics benchmark (R-Bench) designed to evaluate robot-oriented video generation across five task domains and four distinct embodiments. It assesses both task-level correctness and visual fidelity through reproducible sub-metrics, including structural consistency, physical plausibility, and action completeness. Evaluation of 18 representative models reveals substantial room for improvement in generating physically realistic robot behaviors, with a Spearman correlation coefficient of 0.96 between R-Bench scores and human evaluations, confirming its effectiveness. To address the limitations of existing robot datasets in diversity, scale, and fidelity, we introduce a refined four-stage data pipeline, resulting in RoVidX-4M, the largest open-source high-quality robotic dataset for video generation, which covers thousands of tasks with comprehensive physical property annotations. Together, R-Bench and RoVidX-4M provide a unified foundation for evaluating and advancing video foundation models in robotic contexts, paving the way for the pursuit of general intelligence. All data and code will be open-sourced.
